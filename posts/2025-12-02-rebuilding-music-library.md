For a long time, music was something I didn‚Äôt just listen to. I lived *with* it.

## Streaming and the quiet erosion of attention
I‚Äôm not anti-technology. I love good tools. But the shift to streaming did something subtle to my connection with music.

I‚Äôd like to reintroduce a slower kind of discovery.

## LPs, rituals and limits
A few years ago, I tried to fix this by going back to vinyl.

# A tiny player with real buttons
The other key piece was getting a dedicated music player again.

I bought a Snowsky Echo Mini and bumped its storage with a 256 GB microSD card. Fifteen years after my last iPod shuffle retired, I have a small object in my hand that is *just* for playing music. No notifications, no messages, no browsers. Just files and buttons.

# Yes, it takes more effort
Is this more work than opening Spotify and tapping on a personalised playlist? Absolutely.

Because I touched it. I decided to include it. I know which folder it lives in, which track feels like the core, and where I first listened to it. The friction makes me care again.
Do I feel more connected to the music I listen to now? Hell yes!

This isn‚Äôt a manifesto against streaming. I‚Äôll probably still use it for certain things: quick checks, background playlists, sharing links. But next to that, I want to slowly re-grow a small, dense, personal music library that I handle with my own hands.
